A THANKSGIVING MESSAGE
by Erin Hannon

It's easy to forget what the holidays are really about. What with all travel plans to make - take this city bus here, hitch hike there - it's easy to get distracted. So I just want to take a minute to say what I'm thankful for this Thanksgiving.
  • I have one of the best jobs in the world, second to only the people that work for Disney On Ice.
  • That really sick stray cat that I used to feed stop coming around, I'm thankful that it looks like he found a permanent home.
  • The McRib is back - making high quality meat available for all.
  • Wolves are making a comeback in Yellowstone.
  • The DA said I no longer have to testify.
  • Houses are real cheap to buy, that's good for everyone, right?
  • My Chia Pet is flourishing.
  • And lastly, they just opened a Big Lots! by my house.
I hope you all feel as blessed as I do. Have a happy Thanksgiving!! Gobble gobble!
KELLY 411
by Kelly Kapoor

Okay, technically I am a colored girl, so I was totally looking forward to catching Tyler Perry's new film, For Colored Girls. Now the movie isn't bad, but it is missing some things that I've come to expect from a movie about colored women. First off, where the hell was Angela Bassett? This is the first black-girls-are-doin'-it-on-their-own movie that she wasn't a part of. What's Love Got To Do With It, How Stella Got Her Groove Back, and Waiting to Exhale were religious experiences for me. I totally missed her in this. On the plus side the movie does star Janet - Ms. Jackson if you're nasty - BUT she doesn't look like Janet Jackson and she doesn't sound like Janet Jackson, gone is her signature Michael Jackson voice. Weird. I'd say the most disappointing aspect of this movie is that it doesn't have a killer soundtrack. There is no awesome Mary J. Blige song about dumping some deadbeat man because he's all drama. There isn't even a song from Janet! Soundtracks are the best part of movies about black girls (hello, Dream Girls?) so the fact that there is not one song in this movie that makes me want to wave a finger is let down. Ugh, this movie could have been so much better!

Note to Tyler Perry, next time you want to make a movie for colored girls, I suggest you consult this colored girl for some tips. Maybe you can make a movie about a customer service/minority exec that works at a paper company and marries Usher after a long make-over montage? Think about it Tyler. :-)
MEREDITH'S GOT SOMETHIN' TO SAY
by Meredith Palmer

So the FDA is now puttin' these new warning labels on the cigarettes packages that show photos of dead bodies and screwed up teeth. I hate 'em. Once again the government is forgettin' all the positive aspects of cigarettes. Without Camel Cash and Malbro Miles I wouldn't have any Christmas gifts for my kid. Another good thing about cigarettes is that they help you unwind. I'm a single mother with awful teenager, crappy job, and bad credit - I depend on my smokes to take the edge outta my day. I especially like puffing on Camels, so much so that the teenagers around the block call me Camel. Cigarette breaks are one of my few joys in life, so I don't wanna see some buzz killing photo of a dead guy whenever I pull out a carton. The government needs to mind their business and lay off!

A POEM
by Kevin Malone

"Paula Deen"

Oh Paula Deen,
You are the fairest cook that I have seen.
Your pigs in a blanket make me swoon,
You make me blush when you lick your spoon.

I watch your show with an ice cold Miller,
You're books are better than anything by Author Miller.
As a cook you always deliver,
You're mac & cheese recipe makes my lip quiver.

I love the way you fry a Twinkie,
I love that your portions are never dinky.
My affections for you, Paula, are here to stay,
I even love you more than Rachael Ray.

SCOTT'S SHOTS
by Michael Scott

Let's get critical! It's been a while since I've done my "WATCH THIS!" movie review in the newsletter, so I thought it was time to give you my thoughts on what's playing in a theater near you.
  • Megamind - More like Mega Good! I loved it! The title character isn't the only genius involved with this movie, so are the people that drew it. Two thumbs and big toes way up!
  • Due Date - More like Dude Date! 2 Guys + Car + Road Trip = hilarious, always! Planes, Trains and Automobiles was sidesplitting. Tommy Boy was epic. Dumb and Dumber was hysterical. Rain Man had comedic moments, mostly when Dustin Hoffman talked in that funny voice. Now joining them in the ranks of awesome road trip movies is Due Date. Make a date...with Due Date.
  • Morning Glory - More like Morning Boring. Didn't see it. Not my kind of chick flick.
  • For Colored Girls - More like Four Colored Girls. Actually there's like Eight African American Women. Madea is not one of them. :-( (When I first saw Loretta Devine on the screen I thought she was Madea, but she wasn't). However, it is does star the great Claire Huxtable, so I liked it. Mmmm'kay Girrrrrrl!
  • Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows Part 1 - This movie's really great! I didn't read the last book because I didn't want them to spoil this movie for me - so glad I did! AHH can't wait for Part Deux!!
  • The 3rd Floor - Too scary! It gave me nightmares for a week. I still can't be around Erin without thinking she's going to kill me. This movie should only be watched by serial killers and teenage boys.
