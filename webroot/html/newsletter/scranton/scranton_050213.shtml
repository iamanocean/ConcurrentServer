Please Read the Following
by: Dwight Schrute


Now that I'm manager, I am going to expect the most out of my employees, so here is a list of what you should have in or around your desk everyday.




  • One outfit for work - business attire, One outfit for morning exercises - business casual, three outfits for doomsday scenario - camouflage, hazmat, and casual
  • Three number two pencils sharpened at all times
  • Two pens; one blue, one black. (those pens that have multiple colors only count as one pen.)
  • A compass - for drafting purposes
  • One calculator
  • One abacus to calibrate your calculator
  • One flashlight
  • One set of jumper cables
  • One tourniquet
  • One bottle of IPECAC (6 or 8 oz)
  • One backup pair of shoelaces (black or brown)
  • One home/office tracheotomy kit
  • One jar sunscreen (for use during fire drills)
  • Batteries sizes AAA, AA, A, D, C, and Car.
  • One bottle of water to stay hydrated at all times
  • One personal defense device (See me if you don't have anything)
  • One compass - for navigational purposes


Anyone found to be lacking any of these items will be reprimanded.

Mother's Day
by: Dwight Schrute


As many of you know, my mother has been deceased for some time now, but with Mother's Day approaching I'd like to give her one final gift. With Mose's help, I will be secretly exhuming her body under the cover of night from the fancy cemetery and re-interring her in the family cemetery on Schrute Farms. I'll also be pre-digging plots for myself and my girlfriend Esther. I know what you're thinking. If I'm doing something as romantic as digging a grave for my girlfriend, I must be getting ready to propose soon. Well, you're wrong. The side-by-side graves will be strictly platonic. Anyone who is interested in helping, please meet near reception after work on the Friday preceding Mother's Day. You may want to practice exhuming a few graves before then. But be careful, some cemeteries frown on it. Shovels will be provided. Please wear comfortable shoes. Mom, it will be good to see you again.

Dwight Schrute: Master of the Universe
by: Dwight K. Schrute

Most of you watched helplessly as I dominated the paper airplane contest last month. Once again, I just want to say, "Eat it, suckers!" If you don't like the taste of losing then you shouldn't be so bad at everything. In all seriousness though, the prize money is going to a good cause. My cousin Mose recently got himself into a bit of financial trouble after his online girlfriend turned out to be a scam to steal his identity. Thank goodness Mose doesn't have a Social Security Number, but by the time I found out he had already mailed away all the money in his banking jar which he was saving to buy a new set of brooms. Mose is still insistent on going to meet his fake girlfriend in Central Park next Valentine's Day like they promised. Maybe for just one magical day, Susie Cornhole will be a real girl and not two Croatian guys who put pictures of themselves holding guns in their emails.



Dwight's Corner
by: Dwight Schrute

And now on a personal note, I just wanted to thank everyone for finally getting out of my way, allowing for my meteoric rise to the manager's chair. From being hired by Ed Truck to being the Assistant Regional Manager under Michael, to working under that idiot Charles Miner, to that even bigger idiot Jim Halpert. Thank goodness they had the sense to only make him a co-manager. Then there was Deangelo, Andy, Nellie, and then Andy again. It's been a long journey. I've put up with a lot of crap to get into this chair. And now that I'm finally here, I can say, it was worth it. I've learned much from my predecessors. Mostly, be competent, don't be an idiot, and beware of excessively showy stunts like trying to dunk a basketball from the free throw line. So thank you for all you've taught me, former Regional Managers of Dunder Mifflin. And for the rest of you, thank you for not having any ambition, or at least having enough foresight to not stand between me and my destiny. The era of "Großen Sprungs von Papier vorwärts" has begun! Together we shall rewrite the history of paper! That wraps up this month's newsletter. I hope you paid attention. You will be quizzed on this.
