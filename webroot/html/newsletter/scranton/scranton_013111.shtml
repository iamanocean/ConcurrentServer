CELEB 411
by Kelly Kapoor

Peoples' Choice, Critics' Choice, Globes, SAGs, Oscars, BAFTAs. What does it mean? BAD FASHION. I mean obviously there are always a few perfect tens (i.e. Catherine Zeta, Chris Pine), but lately it seems starlets are losing their minds. Take the Golden Globes for example: Jennifer Love Hewitt wore a wedding gown, Helena Bonham Carter had on two different colored shoes, Michelle Williams was in a sun dress...WTF you guys?! Did you not realize there'd be cameras? Were you drunk? I feel like you were drunk. But honestly I don't even blame the celebrities. I blame the designers. Like excuse me Valentino, how could you let Mariah Carey walk out of your store with that awful navy blue disaster she wore to the Oscars last year? And more importantly, why did you make it?? You have to understand that awards season equals emotional overload for stars. They might wander into your store all hyped up on Pilates and tofu and think, "Oh, this dress is the same color as my skin. I should get it." Or, "This looks like a really cool prom gown that emphasizes my worst parts. I'll take it!" Don't let them!! And just don't even make ugly stuff! Yes, sometimes it's entertaining to ridicule the super mean stars who throw phones at their assistants' heads and stuff, but mostly it just breaks my heart to see them dressed like weirdoes. Anyway, here's to hope for the future. Don't let me down, Academy.
THE FINAL COUNTDOWN
by Ryan Howard

Less than eleven months until the end of the world. I've been putting my apocalyptic bucket list in order, and I think it's pretty great so far. First, I'm going to spend all my savings on a bedroom roof that opens up like a convertible top. Always wanted one. Then I'm going to do some awesome spur-of-the-moment stuff, like go to a Talib Kweli concert at a jazz club, pierce my right ear just to throw people off, drive to Portland, start smoking cigars... But most importantly, I'm going to find myself. I hope you guys do too.

IMPROV TO IMPROVE
by Michael Scott

Improvisation is probably one of the most fun things to do in the world. You can instantly become any character you like: an old blind lady who drives with both feet, a hippie who goes to parties in the desert, a cat, a high school prom queen with body dysmorphia, a ravishingly handsome Greek man who's looking to grow his small business...The possibilities are endless. As a manager, I've always felt that improv skills help me to act quicker on my feet. You think Prison Mike or Date Mike or Mykonos were just accidents? Pure luck? No way. Those were the result of spending hours upon hours - a third of my life really - practicing improvisation. Here are a few beginners tips, in case you want to bring a little improv into your own lives:
  • Add some info and backstory. If someone tells you you're spaz, say yes you are, and that you've been to spaz school and have a Masters in spazzing. Suddenly you have a scene full of stimulating facts.
  • Do things you wouldn't do in the real world. Take risks! Be it at the comedy club or in the conference room, your audience is there because they want to escape reality, and your job is to entertain them. Normally you wouldn't drop a line to Angela about how it looks like she borrows her clothes from that show Big Love. But during improv, anything goes. And since you're just playing a character, the audience always understands and laughs with you.
  • Above all else, have fun! Remember that's the point of improvisation. When I'm stuck in a scene and don't know what to do next, I make crazy interesting choices. For example -- kissing Oscar in front of everyone to show that I'm accepting of the gay man and all he has to offer. That was entertaining, enjoyable (for him), and really engaging. Live it, love it, improv! You just got served...a lesson in improv. Love, Michael.
