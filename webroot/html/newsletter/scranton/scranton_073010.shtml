OSCAR'S GRIPE
by Oscar Martinez

I would like to note that we are professionals and so I'd expect that our grammar be professional, i.e. use words correctly. Over the last couple days I've heard many atrocities toward the English language and my ears. I'd like to take a moment to address three such offenses.
  • Yesterday I heard Kelly Kapoor saying, "OMG, the smell of Toby's ear medicine is making me nauseous." Nauseous is the cause; nauseated is the feeling. Kelly should have said, "OMG, the smell of Toby's ear medicine is making me nauseated."

  • Michael Scott said "Just ate a bunch of potato pancakes, I'm going to lay down in my office for a couple hours." It should be, "...lie down in my office" you lay objects, but when you rest, you lie.
  • Tuesday I overheard Stanley say, "Andy, I am disinterested in hearing about your uncle's funeral." He should have said, "Andy, I am uninterested in hearing about your uncle's funeral." Disinterested is impartial, uninterested means you have no interest.
Please take note of these corrections; sparing me from hearing these grammatical affronts will save me from being nauseated. Thank you for your time.
