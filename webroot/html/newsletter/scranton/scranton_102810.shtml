DON'T BE FRIGHTENED OF THE RULES
by Gabe Lewis

Attention Doomder Mifflin Scarebre: I want you all to have a hair-raising Halloween this year! Mwuah-ha-ha-ha-ha-ha-ha-ha. But everyone's first concern should be safety. Here is a short list of standard rules you should abide by:
  1. Please make sure your costume is fire proof and/or treated with fire retardant. Let's maintain a "No-Burn" policy together.
  2. If your costume includes a mask, ensure that the eyeholes are large enough for reasonable peripheral vision. Although Michael Myers was a serial killer, he always got his work done.
  3. Do not ingest any dry ice, no matter how funny and entertaining it may seem to others.
  4. Limit the amount of treats you eat as not to experience a sugar high while at work. I suggest keeping a treat log to monitor yourself. Make a neat game out of it!
  5. Keep the Jack o' Lanterns away from all curtains. Sometimes they get hungry. Just kidding! It's a serious fire hazard. Again, "No-Burn."
Cheers! Or should I say...fears.
CELEB 411: I'M THE CELEB!
by Kelly Kapoor

Beyonce, Mariah Carey, Hilary Duff, J-Lo. What do I have in common with them? Wrong question. It should be, what do they have in common with me? Answer: Fame. We all sing, we all act... Wait, did she say "act?" Yes that's right you guys! Coming to a theater near you sometime soonish is ME, KELLY KAPOOR, starring in my very first film directed by the super talented Ryan Howard. It's called THE 3RD FLOOR and it's amay-to-the-zing!

After the success of Subtle Sexuality, Erin and I went back to the studio to lay down some new tracks. We ate a lot of ice cream, drank a ton of Red Bull and got mad inspired. But for some reason we weren't feeling it. I thought of the immortal words of Hilary Duff. She said, "I feel like such a normal person, yet everybody around the world knows my music, my movie, my name and my face. It's a good feeling." Even though I didn't know what movie she was talking about, that's when it dawned on me - singing might lead to stardom, but not superstardom. And why be a star if you're not going to be a superstar? Why be in a porn if you're not going to be a porn star? What's the point of being a model if you're not going to be a supermodel? I decided to up the ante, and had the ambition and courage to act in Ryan's movie. Haters are saying he only put me in it because I'm his GF, but we were broken up at the time so ha! Being the star of THE 3RD FLOOR will totally bring my career to new heights, and I am so ready ;)

FRUITS WOULD TAKE NO MORE LABOR
by Angela Martin

You know what scares me on Halloween? Fat children. Twelve percent of Pennsylvania's children are considered to be obese. That is a shocking number. My heart and my tears go out to them, and my malevolent thoughts go out to their parents. I would like you all to think twice before you load up your trick-or-treat dishes with peanut brittle and Zagnuts, and consider the nutritive value of fruits and vegetables. That razor blade in the apple story scared many people away from handing out and accepting healthy treats, but it was purely a myth. You know what is actually inside of an apple? An abundance of vitamins and minerals. Hopefully by next Halloween I won't see any true-to-size oompa loompas or overstretched S's on Superman costumes.

EXORCISM BASICS
by Creed Bratton

All Hallows Eve is a real freaky night, man. It's the one time of year when the line between this world and the Otherworld becomes thin, allowing weirdo spirits to pass through. Naturally, lots of people get possessed. Here's a Do's and Don'ts list for an exorcism in case you need to perform one, or have one performed on you:
  • DO be hydrated.
  • DON'T be unclear on how you feel about evil.
  • DO be clear about who is the exorcist and who is the excisee.
  • DO wear loose-fitting clothing.
  • DO bring popcorn for afterward (you'll be ravenous).
  • DON'T speak in Latin - it's cliché.
  • DON'T have a dead mother.
  • DON'T call me if you need any help. I prefer texts.
REAL IMPORTANT REMINDER
by Meredith Palmer

I'd like to make it known that I invented the concept of dressing sexy on Halloween. Hell I even invented dressing slutty on Christmas. But now it seems that every broad in town dresses up like a sexy streetwalker come October 31st, and nobody's makin' a big deal out of Meredith's nurse costume with a hole cut out for the boobs anymore. I'm totally fine with it, all I'm asking for is some credit. Before you strap on your sexy Michelle Obama costume, just remember the little lady who paved the way.
