FROM: Robert California

TO: All Dunder Mifflin Sabre Employees

SUBJECT: Binghamton - Just Like My Marriage, It's Over

Dear Colleagues,

May 2, 2012 (3:14 A.M.) - This evening I celebrated the finalization of my divorce with the ardency of someone like Charlie Sheen before he hired his sober coach, or Gerard Butler after he left the Betty Ford Center and was getting his groove back with loose women in Porta-Johns at the Coachella Music Festival. What I'm trying to say is it's been an outrageous night. Right now I'm wearing only one sock and a handwoven Mexican blanket. At this point I can't be sure about the single sock thing... Oh wait, I've just spotted the other one! Evidently I used it as a koozie for my bottle of wine. My goodness, I'm amusingly charming sometimes. I purchased the Mexican blanket many years ago from a young woman in the sleepy village of Tulum. She called it a "serape." I called her beautiful. We necked for hours. I regularly use the serape to meditate on in the nude, which I just finished doing moments ago as a way to wind down and lower cortisol production before bed. This inebriated introspection allowed me to become very aware of my present life, and to hone in on what is not working. And that, my dear friends, is the Binghamton branch.

This business is like a sand painting. It changes with the wind. As Hegel said, "The owl of Minerva flies at dusk." Too late we learned that your branch cannot be saved. I am suddenly crying my eyes out as I type this, tears of euphoria at the braveness of my honesty. SHUT IT DOWN EFFECTIVE IMMEDIATELY!!!!! Your branch is not profitable; that's a fact, and now we must all sleep.

Infinite love,
Robert California

PS - Has anyone seen my wallet?
